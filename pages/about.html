About Kerio Valley

The geography, the people, and the vision behind this site

The Valley

Kerio Valley is a section of the Great Rift Valley located in the western highlands of Kenya, spanning Elgeyo-Marakwet, Baringo, and Tugen Hills counties. The valley stretches roughly 80 kilometres, flanked on the west by the dramatic Elgeyo Escarpment and on the east by the Tugen Hills.

The Kerio River runs the length of the valley floor, fed by streams that tumble down from the escarpment. Its banks are lined with acacia woodland, riverine forest, and crocodile-inhabited pools, forming a rich ecological corridor.

At the top of the escarpment sits Iten, a town perched at 2,400 m above sea level — world-famous as the training base of Kenya's greatest distance runners, including Olympic and World Championship medallists.

The People

The valley is home to the Elgeyo (also called Keiyo) and Marakwet communities on the escarpment, and the Tugen and Njemps peoples on the valley floor and eastern hills.

The Marakwet are renowned for an ancient, intricate irrigation system — furrows carved into the escarpment that channel water from highland streams down to valley farms, some over 500 years old and still in use today.

Hospitality is a cornerstone of valley culture. Visitors are welcomed with local sorghum porridge, goat meat, and the sound of traditional songs echoing across the hills.
