The Iten Viewpoint

Standing on the edge of Africa — 1,000 metres above the Kerio Valley floor

The View That Stops You

On the western edge of Iten town, the land simply ends. The plateau drops away in a sheer wall of rock and red earth — the Elgeyo Escarpment — and suddenly the entire Kerio Valley spreads before you, 1,000 metres below and 60 kilometres wide.

The Iten Viewpoint is the single most dramatic vantage point along the entire escarpment rim. The valley floor below is a patchwork of green irrigation plots, the silver thread of the Kerio River, and the rust-red soil of the rift. On clear mornings the Tugen Hills rise from the far side, and the haze of the valley stretches all the way to the horizon.

It is not a managed tourist site with ticket booths or guardrails — it is simply a pull-off at the road's edge where locals park motorbikes, runners stretch after their morning intervals, and visitors stand in silence with nowhere else they would rather be.

Getting There

The viewpoint is on the western edge of Iten town, just off the main road that runs through the centre. It is a short walk — or a brief boda-boda ride — from anywhere in town.

  • From Eldoret: 45 minutes by matatu or private car (~40 km)
  • From Kabarnet: 1.5 hours by matatu (~70 km via Tambach)
  • From Nairobi: 5–6 hours by road via Nakuru - Eldoret
  • Ask any boda-boda rider in Iten for the "viewpoint" — everyone knows it

Visitor Tips

  • Best time: Early morning (6 – 8 am) for clear air and golden light.
  • Weather: Iten is cool year-round — bring a layer, especially in the morning.
  • Photography: Face west at sunrise, face east for valley shots midday.
  • Respect: This is a working road used by runners and locals daily. Park carefully and give way.
  • Stay: Several guesthouses in Iten are within walking distance — book ahead during peak training season.
  • Combine it: Pair with a descent down Biretwo Road for a full escarpment experience.

While You're in Iten

The viewpoint is just the beginning — the town has more to offer.

Kenya's premier athletics training facility. Watch world-record holders train at dawn on the red-dirt tracks around town.
